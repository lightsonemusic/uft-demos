🍂 Mulching Guide - Integral Syntropic Protocol

What is Mulching in Syntropic Agriculture?

🌍 Live Soil Cover
10-25cm layer of organic matter (leaves, pruned branches, compost) spread over soil.
🔄 NPK Cycling
Mulch decomposes slowly = continuous nutrition. Pruned branches become system's own fertilizer.
💧 Water Conservation
Reduces evaporation 50-70%. Keeps soil cool and naturally moist.
🛡️ Soil Protection
Prevents compaction, protects microorganisms, suppresses weeds.

Types of Mulch Recommended

🍂 Dry Leaves
Oak, Beech, Chestnut. Decompose quickly. Start with 10-15cm.
🌿 Selective Weeding
Spontaneous plants with nutrients. Non-compacting. Add biodiversity.
🪵 Pruned Branches
From dynamic pruning. Cut 5-10cm. Decompose in 6-12 months = slow fertilizer.
♻️ Partial Compost
Half-fermented residues. Accelerates microbiota. Mix with dry leaves.

Mulching Protocol: When & How Much

🌱 Step 1: Prepare Soil
Dig 40-50cm hole. Add compost base at bottom.
🌿 Step 2: Plant
Place plant. Water well. Firm soil.
🍂 Step 3: Mulch Immediately
Spread 10-15cm mulch around (leave 5cm from trunk).
🔁 Step 4: Maintenance
Every 6 months, add new layer. Mulch decomposes = needs replenishing.
